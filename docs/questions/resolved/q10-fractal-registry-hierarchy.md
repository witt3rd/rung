# Q10 — Is the registry structure fractal? Does the opfibration iterate up a domain hierarchy?

**Status:** RESOLVED (2026-07-19, two independent CT reviews converge completely) · **Verdict:** the registry is genuinely fractal — an **iterated Grothendieck opfibration of dependent optics**. The vertical domain hierarchy and the horizontal sibling-loop are *the same categorical object at two scales*. The **theorem** is resolved; the **application** (building the `relational-being/` registry hierarchy) stays gated by the third-instance rule.

## The question

Q9 resolved the dependency superstructure as a **Grothendieck opfibration** `p : E → B` — the *horizontal* structure: typed edges *within one base*, sibling items composing (the three sibling registries composing across repos). Donald named a second, **vertical** axis: a hierarchy of *domains* (`relational-being ⊐ {memory, actions, …}`), each carrying its own registry, where "the layer below (memory) has implications (opfibration) for the layer above (relational beings)." Mapped onto Q9 one meta-level up, the proposal was **an opfibration whose fibres are opfibrations** — and the sharp question was whether that is real structure or only rhymes. The four sub-questions, tee'd for the Q9 bar:

> (a) is the composite a **composition of Grothendieck fibrations**? (b) Is it the **reserved Level-2 slot** of the growth tower, or an *iteration of Level 1*? (c) Does **transport of typed obligations** (Q9's dependent-optic backward pass) compose across meta-levels with the *same* machinery? (d) Are the **horizontal** (sibling composition) and **vertical** (domain hierarchy) axes genuinely the *same opfibration at different scales* — the formal content of "fractal" — or do they differ in a way the analogy hides?

## Resolution

**Both reviews converge completely on all four sub-questions** — a cleaner convergence than Q9, which had one orientation divergence to resolve. The single divergence here is not on the mathematics but on *application readiness* (below).

**(a) The composite is a single Grothendieck opfibration.** Fibrations — and dually opfibrations — are closed under composition: if `p : E → B` and `q : B → A` are opfibrations, so is `q ∘ p : E → A` (Bénabou; Jacobs, Lemma 1.1.4). Concretely: `q : B → B′` maps items to their parent domains (its fibres are the local registries); `p : E → B` is the Q9 opfibration (states over items). The composite `q ∘ p : E → B′` is the whole tower `domain ⊐ sub-domain ⊐ item ⊐ state` as **one** cohesive opcartesian structure. "An opfibration whose fibres are opfibrations" collapses mathematically into a single unified opfibration. **No extra structure is required** — this is near-routine, a citation not a discovery (which is exactly why the reviewer-weight guide flagged it as scaffolding).

**(b) It is an iteration of Level 1 — the Level-2 slot stays reserved.** Opfibrations are 1-morphisms in the 2-category **Cat** and compose by ordinary functor composition; stacking domains stacks 1-categorical bricks. The composite is again an ordinary functor `E → B′` that happens to be an opfibration — objects, morphisms, opcartesian lifts, exactly Level-1 data. No natural transformation is introduced by merely composing opfibrations. **The reserved Level-2 slot of `_map.md` remains vacant.** A genuine Level-2 structure would require a 2-cell — a natural transformation *between* entire fibrations: a dynamic rewrite rule over the dependency graph, a systemic schema migration of the registry itself, or a fibered functor mapping one instance's registry topology to another's. Domain nesting is strictly recursive Level 1; the fractal claim lives *entirely inside* iterated Level 1.

**(c) Obligation-transport composes across meta-levels with one machinery.** Each vertical edge is already a dependent optic (Q9). Optics are strictly closed under composition (Capucci–Hedges; Spivak's *Poly*): the forward pass of the composite is the composite of the individual pushforwards `g_! ∘ f_!`, which coincides with the opcartesian lift of the composite base morphism because lifts compose. The backward pass is successive query-and-cost — each fibre answers from its *current* state, returning a typed obligation that becomes the input view for the next higher fibre — so the exposure vector of the path equals the support of the single composite optic (the chain rule up the composite lens). **`_reach.py` and its typed-exposure successor require zero architectural modification to cross domain boundaries:** the transport algorithm does not need to know whether an edge crosses a sibling boundary or a domain boundary. Same functorial backward pass at every scale.

**(d) Horizontal and vertical are the same structure — the duality is exact.** Under the Grothendieck construction (`∫`), an indexed hierarchy of dependency graphs (vertical) flattens strictly into a single total dependency graph (horizontal). To the category theory there is no structural difference between a horizontal edge (sibling composition) and a vertical edge (child domain implying parent): both are generating morphisms in the composite base, lifting via identical opcartesian pushforwards. The vertical axis (`memory ⊏ relational-being`) is *base morphisms* in the higher opfibration `q`; the horizontal axis (sibling mutual-loop) is morphisms *within a fibre* of `q`. Both are the same uniform construction — same base-generation rule (free category on a typed graph), same fibre assignment (each object carries a lower-level opfibration), same lift semantics (typed opcartesian pushforwards / dependent optics), same absorption of advisory non-determinism via the Q7 coproduct. **Self-similarity is strict, not analogy:** each fibre of a vertical opfibration *is* a horizontal opfibration of the layer below. The registry pattern is recursive by construction — an iterated Grothendieck opfibration of dependent optics.

### The one divergence — theorem vs. application

The reviews split on exactly one thing, and it resolves cleanly by distinguishing *what was proven* from *what it licenses*.

- **Review 2** (optics): "You are unblocked to build `relational-being/` + nested `memory/`, `actions/` — you are not inventing use to fill a slot; you are deploying an established Level-1 mechanism that just cleared the third-instance rule."
- **Review 1** (fibrations): the fractal clause "remains a *description* of the model, not a mandate to build the hierarchy until a genuine multi-domain cascade appears."

**Resolution toward Review 1 — and this is the crux.** The reviews prove a **theorem** (fibrations compose; optics compose; the Grothendieck construction flattens). A theorem does not need a third *lived instance* — two independent reviews on the mathematics is the bar, exactly as Q9 resolved the opfibration on the math without waiting for three cascades. So **the categorical claim is resolved.** But there is still only **one** lived instance of the vertical structure (memory ⊏ relational-being). Building `relational-being/` + `memory/` + `actions/` registries *now*, on the strength of the math, is precisely "inventing the use to fill the slot" that `_map.md`'s third-instance rule forbids. Review 2 conflates "the theorem cleared the bar" with "the third-instance rule cleared" — those are different gates. The theorem being sound is what makes the eventual build *safe*; it is not what makes it *earned*. **The math resolves; the application stays blocked until a second genuine domain hierarchy appears in lived work.**

(The original doc's framing — "the third-instance rule forbids resolving the structure" — made the same conflation in the other direction: it held the *theorem* hostage to lived instances. The reviews correct this. A theorem resolves on proof; an application waits on need.)
